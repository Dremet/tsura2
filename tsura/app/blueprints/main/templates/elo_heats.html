What is Elo?  Originally designed for chess, Elo converts results into rating changes so a single number approximates skill.

Multi-player adaptation.  For each race, every driver has an expected finishing position computed from all current ratings; the gap between expectation and reality becomes an Elo gain or loss.

Update cycle.  Ratings refresh automatically a few seconds after a TripleHeat race.  If you skip a race, your rating remains unchanged.

Limitations.  Because Elo needs several data points to stabilise, we list only drivers with at least three participations. Car-specific skill and other nuances are not yet separated, so treat the rating as an approximate but useful indicator.
